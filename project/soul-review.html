Soul: "Finding Your Purpose, But With A Jazzy Twist"

★★★

11/5/2023

Where to Watch? Disney+

Soul, directed by Pete Docter and Kemp Powers, is a visually stunning and philosophically profound animated film that takes audiences on a journey through the realms of existence, exploring the meaning of life, passion, and the essence of being.

The film follows Joe Gardner, a middle school music teacher and aspiring jazz musician, voiced by Jamie Foxx. After a near-death experience, Joe finds himself in the Great Before, a place where souls develop their personalities before being born on Earth. Desperate to return to his life, Joe teams up with a reluctant soul named 22, voiced by Tina Fey, and together they embark on an adventure that challenges their perspectives on purpose and the pursuit of passion.

One of the film's strengths lies in its exceptional animation and visual design. Pixar once again proves its mastery in creating breathtaking landscapes and character designs. The contrast between the vibrant, bustling streets of New York City and the ethereal, abstract world of the Great Before is nothing short of mesmerizing. The animation seamlessly captures the emotions and nuances of the characters, adding depth to the narrative.

Soul delves into existential questions with a delicate touch, exploring themes that resonate with both adults and children. The film encourages viewers to reflect on the value of life, the pursuit of dreams, and the importance of finding joy in the journey rather than fixating on a singular destination. It cleverly weaves these profound concepts into a story accessible to all ages, making it a truly universal and timeless piece.

The voice performances are stellar, with Jamie Foxx and Tina Fey delivering standout performances that bring their characters to life. Foxx's portrayal of Joe Gardner captures the struggle, passion, and resilience of the character, while Fey's 22 provides a perfect foil with her comedic timing and philosophical musings.

The musical score, composed by Jon Batiste, Trent Reznor, and Atticus Ross, enhances the emotional resonance of the film. From the lively jazz compositions that mirror Joe's passion for music to the celestial tones of the Great Before, the music becomes an integral part of the storytelling, heightening the overall viewing experience.

Soul is not just an animated film; it is a cinematic masterpiece that transcends the boundaries of its genre. It tackles profound themes with humor and heart, leaving audiences with a sense of wonder and a newfound appreciation for the beauty of life. Pixar's ability to create films that entertain, educate, and inspire is showcased once again in this emotionally resonant and visually captivating exploration of the human experience.
